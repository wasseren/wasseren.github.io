How We Work

From origination to execution support

Wasseren operates at the intersection of capital, projects, industry and technology translation. The role is not limited to one narrow advisory function. It is to help move the right opportunity, with the right counterparties, into a workable commercial pathway.

1

Origination

Opportunity identification

Identify, screen and frame relevant opportunities across storage, renewable power, industrial decarbonisation and next-generation energy technologies.

  • Source opportunities through investor, developer, industry and research networks.
  • Conduct early commercial screening on strategic fit, market relevance and counterpart quality.
  • Clarify whether an opportunity is primarily a project, market-entry pathway, technology collaboration or capital-matching exercise.
  • Filter out weak or misaligned opportunities before significant time and cost are committed.
2

Alignment

Counterparty fit

Match investors, developers, industrial counterparties, researchers and market needs so the opportunity is strategically coherent before deeper work begins.

  • Bring together the right combination of capital, technical capability, delivery partners and local market knowledge.
  • Align expectations on project scope, commercial logic, risk appetite and likely transaction pathway.
  • Identify where additional legal, engineering, EPC or advisory inputs are needed to build a credible pathway.
  • Reduce friction early by ensuring discussions start from a shared understanding of objectives and constraints.
3

Structuring

Commercial pathway

Support the commercial logic, engagement model and collaboration framework required to move from interest to a credible pathway for diligence or deployment.

  • Help define the appropriate engagement model, whether investment, partnership, mandate, market-entry collaboration or project support.
  • Coordinate commercial framing with legal, technical and delivery-side inputs where needed.
  • Support preparation of materials, capability positioning and discussion structure for qualified counterparties.
  • Create a pathway that is clear enough for diligence, decision-making and next-step commitment.
4

Execution Support

Momentum and coordination

Help maintain momentum through discussions, introductions, capability framing and practical coordination with the relevant technical, industrial and institutional parties.

  • Support live discussions between investors, developers, advisors, engineering parties and delivery partners.
  • Maintain momentum across multiple workstreams so opportunities do not stall between initial interest and practical next steps.
  • Provide a coordinated interface for counterparties entering the Australian market or engaging across unfamiliar stakeholder environments.
  • Help translate strategic intent into an execution-ready engagement pathway.
